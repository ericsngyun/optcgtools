# Model usage policy

- vision-language models classify, explain, and review; they are not trusted for final pixel-accurate masks;
- promptable segmentation proposes semantic regions and propagates them through registered video;
- classical computer vision handles geometry, registration, signal measurement, and deterministic preprocessing;
- inverse-rendering methods initialize or fit material parameters, but outputs must remain interpretable and reviewable;
- raw private captures stay on approved infrastructure;
- all models, weights, prompts, settings, and hashes are recorded;
- confidence and uncertainty are preserved;
- human approval is mandatory before production publication.
