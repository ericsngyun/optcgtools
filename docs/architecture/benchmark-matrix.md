# Benchmark calibration matrix

The first benchmark set should cover distinct material constructions rather than simply the most expensive cards.

| Slot | Family | Candidate reference | Required purpose |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| B01 | rare-holo-standard | authenticated current rare holo | establish lowest-cost foil baseline |
| B02 | sr-foil-basic | authenticated SR | distinguish broad SR foil from coated alt art |
| B03 | alt-art-coated | restrained alternate art | calibrate clearcoat-first treatment |
| B04 | alt-art-textured | visibly textured alternate art | validate card-specific normal and texture maps |
| B05 | manga-prismatic-panel | Shanks OP01-120 or equivalent | establish manga-panel foil and ink suppression |
| B06 | sp-gold-ornamental | Luffy/Teach anniversary-style SP | split gold metallic linework from iridescent fill |
| B07 | sp-manga-scene | Zoro manga-scene SP or equivalent | validate broad patch-like panel response |
| B08 | leader-parallel-textured | textured leader parallel | validate frame, badge, art, and title material groups |
| B09 | treasure-rare | authenticated TR | prevent TR from inheriting SP assumptions |
| B10 | manga-gold-metallic | authenticated gold manga | establish independent gold-manga family |

## Selection rules

- physical card must be authenticated or sourced directly from trusted GenkiStuff inventory;
- English and Japanese variants are separate benchmark entries when print behavior differs or remains unverified;
- select cards with clean surfaces and document condition;
- use at least two cards within any family before declaring a family preset reusable;
- include one dark-art and one light-art example where ink density could change perceived foil behavior;
- record reference-source URLs for research, but do not ingest third-party images as production assets without permission.

## Benchmark completion record

Each slot requires:

- controlled capture session;
- registered frame stacks;
- reviewed semantic regions;
- reviewed material maps;
- fitted research renderer;
- compiled CSS fallback;
- optional GLB where quality tier requires it;
- matched-angle comparison report;
- named reviewer and approval state;
- documented failure modes and unsupported behaviors.
