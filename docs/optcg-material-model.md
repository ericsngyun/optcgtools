# OPTCG material model and validation plan

## Initial visual hypotheses

Observed SP cards do not share one universal finish. The lab therefore starts with families rather than a single `SP` shader.

### SP — etched metallic

Use for cards whose finish reads as metallic linework or engraved foil over selected regions.

- narrow directional spectral bands;
- high-frequency diagonal or radial microtexture;
- moderate saturation;
- strong local highlights but controlled full-card luminance;
- card-specific segmentation.

### SP — rainbow field

Use for cards with broad holographic backgrounds, bubbles, radiating motifs, or full-field diffraction.

- wider spectral bandwidth;
- larger color travel across tilt;
- less aggressive micro-etch;
- still preserve matte artwork where reference footage shows it.

### Alternate art — coated foil

Use for premium cards whose dominant cue is a coated laminate rather than aggressive diffraction.

- broad neutral glare;
- restrained chroma;
- selective metallic accents;
- lower layer opacity.

## Required reference capture

For every card family:

1. Straight-on high-resolution scan in diffuse light.
2. Five-to-ten second video with one small light moving horizontally and vertically.
3. One oblique still at approximately 25–35 degrees.
4. Notes for which regions appear matte, metallic, embossed, or transparent-coated.

## Acceptance criteria

A profile should be rejected when:

- color appears while the physical reference remains neutral;
- the entire illustration foils despite matte regions in the reference;
- glare moves with the foil texture instead of independently;
- black ink becomes gray or washed out;
- highlights clip into large white patches;
- the card only looks convincing at one cursor position.
